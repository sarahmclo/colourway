Colour Theory Overview

Colour Theory

Colour theory is a set of principles and guidelines that helps us understand how colours interact with each other and how they can be combined harmoniously in various visual contexts such as art, design, and even psychology. Understanding colour theory enables artists and designers to make informed decisions about colour palettes, creating impactful and cohesive visual experiences.

Primary, Secondary & Tertiary Colours

Primary Colours: Cannot be created by mixing other colours. Secondary Colours: Created by mixing two primary colours together. Tertiary Colors: Created by mixing a primary colour with a neighboring secondary colour on the colour wheel.

Colour Harmony

This refers to the pleasing combination of colours in a way that is visually appealing. Different colour harmonies include complementary, analogous, and triadic. Colour theory is crucial in various fields, including art, graphic design, interior design, and fashion, as it helps creators evoke specific emotions, convey messages, and create visually appealing compositions.

Colour Temperature

Colours can be categorized as warm or cool based on their perceived temperature. Warm colours evoke feelings of warmth and energy, while cool colours convey a sense of calmness and serenity. Colour Psychology: Colours can also have psychological effects and cultural associations.
