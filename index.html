Personal Style

Understanding colour theory is essential for identifying which colours enhance your natural features and personal style. Warm undertones often complement earthy tones like reds and oranges, while cool undertones may prefer blues and purples. Neutrals serve as versatile staples for any wardrobe, providing a foundation for incorporating accent colours that express your individuality.

Daily Environment

Colour theory plays a key role in interior design, influencing the mood and atmosphere of a space. Warm colours like yellows and oranges can make a room feel cozy and inviting, while cool colours like greens and blues evoke a sense of calm and serenity. By applying colour theory principles, you can transform your home/work into a harmonious space that reflects your taste and style.

Source: 'COLOR THEORY BASICS' by Sarah Renae Clark

Health & Well-being

Colour theory extends beyond aesthetics to impact our health and well-being. Certain colours have been shown to affect mood, energy levels and physical health. Exposure to natural greens and blues can reduce stress to promote relaxation, while vibrant yellows and oranges can boost vim and creativity. Incorporating healing colours into our surroundings can enhance our overall sense of vitality.

Colour Therapy & Workshops

Colour theory forms the basis of colour therapy, a holistic practice that harnesses the power of colour to promote healing and balance. Workshops often explore the psychological and physiological effects of colour, as well as techniques for incorporating them into self-care routines. Through guided exercises and discussion, participants learn how to use colour to enhance and cultivate inner harmony.
